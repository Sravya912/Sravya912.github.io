Movva Venu Gopala Swamy Temple

The Movva Venu Gopala Swamy Temple is a Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Krishna, specifically in his form as Venu Gopala. It is located in the town of Movva in Krishna district of Andhra Pradesh, India.

History Of Movva Venu Gopala Swamy Temple

This temple belongs to Dwapara Yuga and during that period once Sri Krishna danced along with Radha. Anklet bells from his legs were fallen on the earth. To find that bell, Lord Sri Krishna ordered Narada maharshi to find it and bring it back. That bell borned as moudgalya maharishi. Then Narada maharshi explained about the birth of moudgalya maharishi.Then moudgalya maharishi created an Idol of Sri Venugopala Swamy with sand and prayed for Sri Krishna and performed a penance here. The Lord gave darshan to him from that time. This region got the name as movva, later it was named as movva.There are separate shrines for Sri Venugopala Swamy and Godadevi in this temple. Festivals celebrated in Movva Venu Gopala Swamy Temple


  • Sri Krishna Janmashtami
  • Deepavali
  • Vaikunta Ekadasi
  • Vasanthotsavam

Sri Krishna Janmashtami

This is the birthday of Lord Krishna. It is celebrated for nine days starting from Sravana Bahula Ashtami.
1st Day - Vratam
2nd Day - Utti Festival from 7 pm at the Holy Garden.
3rd Day up to 9th Day - The function will be celebrated from 6pm onwards
Prasadams will be distributed to the devotees

Deepavali

On Asweeyuja Bahula Chaturdasi & Amavasya the Lord is taken in procession to the holy garden. An idol of Narakasura is made & burnt as sign of victory of good over evil. On the next day i.e on Diwali day the temple will be illuminated with special lights.

Vaikunta Ekadasi

On this day the Vaikunta Dwara, the way to Heaven (North door) is opened. Devotees entering the temple through that dwara. Temple is treated as Heaven that day (auspiciously). Special Vratam and Annadanam will be performed on that day.

Vasanthotsavam

Vasanthotsavam festival is a celebration of the beauty of nature, symbolizing the rejuvenation and blossoming of life in the season of spring. It is an auspicious time for devotees to express their devotion and seek the divine grace of Lord Venkateswara.

Location
