History Of Arasavalli Sun Temple

Ram temples are adorned with grandeur during the coronation of Lord Rama and the well-being of Sitaram. Shiva temples come alive with fervent devotees on Mahashivratri and during the month of Kartika. Vishnu temples shimmer with exquisite decorations during the Dhanur month, hosting ceremonies unique to their respective months. Yet, in the heart of Uttarandhra Bhadradri, there exists a place that draws devotees all year round, where festivals continue to grace the calendar. This exceptional place is Ramatheerdham, where the union of Shiva and Keshava can be witnessed. Nestled in the Nellimarla mandal of Vizianagaram district in Uttarandhra, Ramatheerdham holds a significant place in the hearts of devotees. Legend has it that Lord Rama resided in this sacred land for a time and chanted the divine mantra of Lord Shiva. It is this unique blend of devotion that attracts worshippers from all corners of the world during the sacred occasions of Shivratri and the month of Kartikam. A monolithic mountain, standing tall at 600 meters and stretching two kilometers, lies to the north of this temple. It is believed that Sita, Rama, and the Pandavas wandered these hallowed grounds. The Koneru, adjacent to the Ram Temple, holds an enchanting quality as its waters never recede. To the west of this water body, you can explore the relics of Bhima's burra, the sacred Gadi fireplace, Buddha statues, Parnashala, Palukurai, the Panchas of the Pandavas, and Seethamma Puriti Coch Taluk.During their exile in the Dwapara Yuga, the Pandavas are said to have traversed this very region. When they requested Lord Krishna to accompany them, He entrusted them with the idols of Sitaramalakshman and advised them to worship these divine forms instead. Historians believe that the presence of Bhima's house in this area serves as evidence of their sojourn. In the 16th century, King Pusapati Sitaramchandra Maharaja, who ruled the region, was visited by a divine dream in which Lord Rama revealed the location of the idols given to the Pandavas during the Dwapara Yuga. These idols were submerged in a water pond atop Bodikonda. The king retrieved the idols and constructed a temple. Thus, the place was named Ramatirdham due to the idols found in the sacred tirtha. Not only is this the oldest temple in the region, but it is also another Bhadradri. In his historical account of Visakhapatnam, written around 1880, Carmichael Dora made mention of this revered place. Historical records indicate that Jains had also made their home in this region. The hills of Gurubhaktakonda and Durgakonda house ancient Buddhist temples, adding to the historical significance of the area. To the north, one can find Nilachalam, and to the west, there is a Jain temple. Additional sanctuaries here include Vaikunthanathaswamy, Venugopalaswamy, Lakshmidevi, Madhavaswamy, Varahalakshmi, Narasimhaswamy, Anjaneyaswamy, Alvarula Sannidhi, and Uma Sadashivaswamy. The footprints of Lord Rama and the impressions of Anjaneyaswamy's feet are still visible on this hill, offering tangible proof of the time Sitarama Lakshmana spent in the Ramatirdham area. Devotees visiting Ram Teertha are encouraged to explore Bhima's house as well.

Festivals celebrated in Arasavalli Temple

  • Sri Ramalingeswara Swamy Kalyanotsavam
  • Sri Rama Navami
  • Brahmotsavam
  • Vaikuntha Ekadasi

Sri Ramalingeswara Swamy Kalyanotsavam

Sri Ramalingeswara Swamy Kalyanotsavam is a significant and elaborate celebration held at various Hindu temples, including the Ramateertham Temple in Andhra Pradesh. This festival marks the divine celestial wedding of Lord Shiva, represented as Ramalingeswara Swamy, and Goddess Parvati. The Kalyanotsavam is a sacred and joyous event, observed with great devotion. It involves beautifully adorned idols of the divine couple, the recitation of Vedic hymns, and special rituals, symbolizing the union of Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati. Devotees witness and participate in this divine wedding, seeking blessings, and spiritual grace. The Kalyanotsavam is a time of immense religious significance and celebration in honor of Lord Shiva's marital union.

Sri Rama Navami

Sri Rama Navami is a significant Hindu festival celebrated in Ramateertham and across India, dedicated to Lord Rama, the seventh incarnation of Lord Vishnu. This auspicious day falls on the ninth day (Navami) of the Chaitra month in the Hindu lunar calendar, typically in March or April. Devotees gather at the Ramateertham Temple to commemorate the birth of Lord Rama with special pujas, recitation of the Ramayana, and processions. The temple comes alive with devotion and spiritual fervor as devotees seek Lord Rama's blessings for righteousness and virtue. Sri Rama Navami is a time of deep reverence and celebration, highlighting the significance of Lord Rama in Hinduism.

Brahmotsavam

The Annual Brahmotsavam in Ramateertham is a prominent religious event celebrated with great fervor. It is an elaborate festival dedicated to the presiding deity of the temple, often Lord Shiva or another prominent deity. The Brahmotsavam typically spans over a week and includes a series of grand processions, pujas, and cultural programs. Devotees from far and wide participate, witnessing the adorned deity and seeking divine blessings. The festivities create a vibrant and spiritually charged atmosphere, strengthening the sense of community and devotion among the worshippers. The Annual Brahmotsavam is a significant occasion that showcases the temple's cultural and religious importance in Ramateertham.

Vaikuntha Ekadasi

Vaikuntha Ekadasi is a highly revered festival celebrated in Ramateertham and many other Hindu temples. This festival usually falls in the month of Margashirsha (December or January) and is dedicated to Lord Vishnu. It holds immense spiritual significance as it marks the opening of the gates to Vaikuntha, Lord Vishnu's celestial abode. Devotees observe fasts and engage in devotional activities, including special pujas, recitation of religious texts, and singing bhajans. The temple premises are beautifully decorated, and a procession of the deity is a common feature. Devotees believe that passing through the Vaikuntha Dwaram (gate) on this day grants them access to the divine realm. Vaikuntha Ekadasi is a day of profound spiritual significance and devotion in Ramateertham.

Diwali

Diwali, known as Deepavali, is celebrated in Ramateertham with great enthusiasm and festivity. The village is adorned with colorful decorations, oil lamps, and decorative lights. Families and friends come together to exchange sweets and gifts, and traditional dishes are prepared and shared. Fireworks light up the night sky, adding to the festive atmosphere. Devotees often visit temples, including the Ramateertham Temple, to offer prayers to the Goddess Lakshmi and seek blessings for prosperity and well-being. Diwali is a time for unity, joy, and the triumph of light and goodness over darkness and ignorance, bringing communities in Ramateertham together in celebration.

Location

Ramateertham is a village panchayat in Nellimarla mandal of Vizianagaram district in Andhra Pradesh in India. It is about 12 km from Vizianagaram city.
